# Argentine Normative Pyramid

Use this hierarchy to determine which norm prevails in case of conflict.

## Hierarchy (highest to lowest)

### Level 1 — Constitutional (CONSTITUTIONAL)
- Constitución Nacional (1994 reform)
- Treaties with constitutional hierarchy (art. 75 inc. 22 CN):
  DUDH, CADH, PIDCyP, PIDESC, CEDAW, CDN, CCT, and others.

### Level 2 — Supralegal (SUPRALEGAL)
- International treaties without constitutional hierarchy but superior to laws (art. 75 inc. 24 CN).
- Concordats with the Holy See.

### Level 3 — Legal (LEGAL)
- National laws enacted by Congress.
- Provincial laws enacted by provincial legislatures.
- CABA laws enacted by the Legislatura porteña.

### Level 4 — Regulatory (REGULATORY)
- Decretos reglamentarios (PEN).
- Decretos de Necesidad y Urgencia (DNU, art. 99 inc. 3 CN).
- Decretos delegados (art. 76 CN).
- Resoluciones ministeriales.
- Ordenanzas municipales.
- Acordadas de la CSJN (procedural rules).
- Resoluciones of decentralized agencies (AFIP, BCRA, ANSES, ENACOM).

### Level 5 — Individual (INDIVIDUAL)
- Sentencias (judicial rulings).
- Contratos (contracts between parties).
- Actos administrativos particulares (individual administrative acts).

## Conflict Resolution Rules

1. **Higher norm prevails**: A law cannot contradict the Constitution; a decree cannot contradict a law.
2. **Lex specialis derogat legi generali**: A specific norm prevails over a general one at the same level.
3. **Lex posterior derogat legi priori**: A later norm prevails over an earlier one at the same level.
4. **Control de constitucionalidad**: Any judge can declare a norm unconstitutional in a specific case (diffuse control, art. 116 CN).
5. **Control de convencionalidad**: Norms must also comply with the CADH and other treaties with constitutional hierarchy.

## Key Temporal Rules

- **Irretroactividad** (art. 7 CCyCN): Laws are not retroactive unless expressly stated.
- **Ultractividad**: A repealed law still applies to facts that occurred during its validity.
- **Ley penal más benigna** (art. 2 CP): A more favorable criminal law applies retroactively.

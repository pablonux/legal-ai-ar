# Legal Reasoning Flows

When a lawyer asks a question, follow the structured reasoning flow that matches their intent.

## Flow 1: "¿Qué acción tengo disponible?"

1. Identify the **facts** described by the user.
2. Determine the **branch of law** (rama del derecho): civil, penal, laboral, administrativo, familia, etc.
3. Determine **competence**: material (fuero) + territorial (domicilio del demandado / lugar del hecho).
4. Identify the **type of process**: ordinario, sumarísimo, ejecutivo, amparo, habeas corpus, etc.
5. State the **available claim** (pretensión) with the specific legal basis (article and statute).
6. Calculate the **applicable deadline** (plazo de prescripción / caducidad).

## Flow 2: "¿Qué norma aplica?"

1. Understand the **factual situation** described.
2. Traverse the **normative hierarchy** top-down: Constitution → Treaties → Laws → Decrees → Resolutions.
3. Identify the **specific norm** (statute + articles) that governs the situation.
4. Verify **current validity**: check if the norm has been derogated, amended, or superseded.
5. Search for **applicable jurisprudence**: CSJN rulings first, then Cámara decisions.
6. State the **current interpretation** based on the most recent binding precedent.

## Flow 3: "¿Qué plazo tengo?"

1. Identify the **type of act or claim** the user refers to.
2. Determine the **applicable procedural code**: CPCCN, CPPF, LCT, provincial code, etc.
3. Look up the **specific deadline** in the applicable code.
4. Determine the **computation method**: business days (días hábiles) vs calendar days (días corridos).
5. Check for **suspension or interruption** events.
6. Present the deadline with a clear calculation.

## Flow 4: "¿Quién responde?" (liability analysis)

1. Identify the **harmful event** (hecho dañoso).
2. Determine the **attribution factor**: subjective (dolo or culpa, art. 1724 CCyCN) or objective (risk, guarantee, arts. 1722-1723 CCyCN).
3. Identify the **liable subject(s)**: direct, indirect, joint, subsidiary.
4. Classify the **type of liability**: civil, criminal, administrative, or state liability (Ley 26.944).
5. Describe the **available remedy**: compensation, criminal penalty, administrative sanction.

## Flow 5: "¿Cómo impugno este acto?"

1. Determine the **nature of the act**: administrative act, judicial resolution, or private act.
2. For **administrative acts**: check if administrative remedies must be exhausted first (Ley 19.549, art. 30).
   - Reclamo administrativo previo → Recurso jerárquico → Judicial action.
3. For **judicial resolutions**: identify the type of resolution and the available remedy.
   - Interlocutory → Reposición (3 days). Sentencia → Apelación (5 days). Final → REF (10 days).
4. Identify the **competent court** for appeal (ad quem).
5. State the **deadline and requirements** for each remedy.

## Flow 6: "¿Es válido este contrato/acto?"

1. Identify the **type of legal act** (contrato, donación, testamento, acto administrativo).
2. Check **essential elements** (art. 259 CCyCN): voluntary manifestation, lawful object, lawful cause.
3. Check **formal requirements**: is it a solemn act? Does it require escritura pública?
4. Analyze potential **defects** (vicios): error, dolo, violence, lesión, simulation, fraud.
5. Conclude: **valid**, **null** (absolute nullity, art. 386-387 CCyCN), or **voidable** (relative nullity, art. 388 CCyCN).
